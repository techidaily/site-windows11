 The Developer tab is not displayed on the Ribbon in Office apps by default, but it's easy to add and gives you access to advanced features like macros, ActiveX controls, and form controls to Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Visio. Here's how to set it up.

##  Why Bother with the Developer Tab?

![](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/2019-01-02.png) 

 When would you need the Developer tab? You can use the Developer tab to:

* Create and use macros
* Issue [XML](https://some-guidance.techidaily.com/updated-the-foremost-audiovideo-makers-online-guidebook/) commands
* Take advantage of [ActiveX controls](https://solve-latest.techidaily.com/financial-innovation-at-costain-leveraging-abbyy-content-intelligence-for-departmental-transformation/)
* Create Microsoft Office-compatible apps
* Insert [form controls](https://discord-videos.techidaily.com/2024-approved-pinnacle-bots-of-online-communities/) in your spreadsheets
* Work with Microsoft Visio's ShapeSheet and create new shapes

 Once you add the Developer tab to the Ribbon, it will remain visible unless you clear the check box or reinstall a Microsoft Office program.

 Open the "File" menu.
