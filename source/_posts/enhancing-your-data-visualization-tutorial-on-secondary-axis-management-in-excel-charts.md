### Key Takeaways

 You can add a secondary axis in Excel by making your chart a combo chart, enabling the "Secondary Axis" option for a series, and plotting the series in a style different from the primary axis. If you decide to remove the second axis later, simply select it and hit Delete.

 If you [have a chart](https://technical-tips.techidaily.com/top-techniques-for-enhancing-photo-quality-on-your-ios-device/) where you're plotting different types of data or the values vary, consider adding a second axis for easier viewing. We'll show you how to painlessly create a two-axis chart in Excel.

Or maybe the values you're displaying vary by greater amounts than the chart can adequately show. In these cases, adding a second vertical axis to the chart can depict the data more effectively.

##  Add a Secondary Axis in Excel

 If you haven't yet created your chart, you can add the secondary axis immediately by [creating a combo chart](https://hardware-tips.techidaily.com/unveiling-the-secrets-of-pc-components-a-deep-dive-by-toms-hardware/) from the start. But if you already have your chart and simply want to add the second axis to it, you'll convert your chart, in a sense, to a combo chart.

Related: [How to Create a Combo Chart in Excel](https://hardware-tips.techidaily.com/unveiling-the-secrets-of-pc-components-a-deep-dive-by-toms-hardware/) 

 To begin the conversion to a two-axis chart, select one of the indicators for the axis you want to turn into a secondary axis. Then, [right-click](https://desktop-recording.techidaily.com/updated-the-art-of-recording-fun-6-techniques-to-document-minecraft-for-2024/) and pick "Change Series Chart Type."

![Change Series Chart Type in the shortcut menu](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/ChangeSeriesChartTypeAddAxis-ExcelSecondaryAxis.png)
